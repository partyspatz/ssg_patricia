I am a MA student of the programm **Interdisciplinary Studies of the Middle East** (ISME) at Freie Universität Berlin. My academic interests are rooted in interdisciplinary perspectives on culture, history, societies and politics in the MENA/WANA region, with a focus on Jewish histories, minority studies as well as studies of peace and conflict resolution.

## My Life

Born in Friedrichshafen at Lake Constance on November 20, 1996, I showed an early interest in cultural traditions, societies and historical contexts.

After my prior BA studies in auditing, I changed my academic track from a focus on economics to one on humanities and indulged into the interdisciplinary field of studying the so-called _Middle East_. 

I completed my BA in Middle Eastern Studies at Martin-Luther-Universität, Halle, in 2025. During this studies program, I had the chance to participate in a study abroad program, which granted me a year-long academic stay at Tel Aviv University. While studying in Tel Aviv, I took courses on 48-Palestinians in Israel, network theory and nationalism & collective memory among others. My experience at Tel Aviv University also sparked my interest in the field of peace and conflict resolution.

 In summer 2026, I finally started my MA studies at Freie Universität Berlin, where I continue to deepen my understanding of contexts and discontinuities within societies in the _Middle East_.

## Current Work

I work as a research assistant for my former Professor, apl. Prof. Dr. Gerold Necker, who is a scholar in the field of kabbalistic studies. His research focuses on late medieval kabbalistic manuscripts currently concentrating on the renowned kabbalist [Moses Zacuto](https://kabbalaheditions.org/projects.en.html) (Moshe Zekhut) who lived in various places of 17th-century Italy. 

Besides this, I enjoy scuba diving, knitting, baking sourdough focaccia and just started learning code - I even built this website myself!

## Research Interests

My personal research focus includes: **48-Palestinians in Israel**, **The May 2021 riots in Israel/Palestine**, **Network Theory**, **Nationalism and Propaganda**, **Peace and Conflict Resolution**, and **Islamophobia**.
